  • Parent-School Communication (pg 5, pg 8)

    • Washington University Nursery School values the partnership between parents and the school, emphasizing open communication, shared values, and mutual respect. Parents are expected to support the school's vision, follow rules, and collaborate constructively, with the understanding that a positive relationship is vital for the child's development.Continuous communication at Washington University Nursery School, achieved through newsletters, the school website, and emails, includes common teacher-initiated phone calls or emails for discussions on a child's adjustment or concerns, with parents encouraged to contact teachers or the director via email or office calls, and teachers remaining accessible after the school day.

  • Discipline (pg 9)

    • The staff and director at WUNS maintain a positive and nurturing environment by establishing necessary classroom limits to ensure order and stimulate each child effectively. Emphasizing safety and positive behavior, the staff is trained to anticipate and address potential issues promptly, with a focus on encouraging self-management and skill development. The discipline policy relies on positive reinforcement, social interaction anticipation, re-direction, validating feelings, and expressing disappointment respectfully, aiming to make school a positive and rewarding experience. In cases of persistent inappropriate behavior, collaboration with parents is sought, and if necessary, recommendations for alternative preschool programs or community services are provided to ensure each child's growth and a suitable learning environment.
