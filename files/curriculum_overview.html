Curriculum Overview

Contains: Educational Philosophy, Social/Emotional Development, Intellectual Development, Physical Development, and Visual Arts.

If looking for parent information (such as communications and discipline), head to the WUNS Parent Guide page.

Highlights

Intellectual Development: Math Skills

At Washington University Nursery School, math skills are honed through activities such as identifying shapes, counting, sorting, and measuring.

Social/Emotional Development

The curriculum at Washington University Nursery School emphasizes the individual strengths and capabilities of each child, fostering self-esteem, communication, responsibility, problem-solving, and collaborative skills within the group setting.

Physical Development: Gross Motor Skills, Fine Motor Skills

Activities like climbing, biking, and playing with balls and sand foster physical development. Fine motor skills are nurtured through writing, drawing, and engaging with activities like bead stringing and collage creation, promoting dexterity and creativity.

Visual Arts

Fostering creativity through visual arts, music, and dramatic play focuses on exploration. A variety of materials and experiences support children's development, with curriculum adjustments based on individual needs.

Extra Help

If children require assistance, the curriculum includes personalized approaches to cater to various learning styles and address specific needs.
