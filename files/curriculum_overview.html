Preview of Contents in the Curriculum Overview

  • Social/Emotional Development

    • The curriculum at Washington University Nursery School emphasizes the individual strengths and capabilities of each child, fostering self-esteem, communication, responsibility, problem-solving, and collaborative skills within the group setting.

  • Intellectual Development: Math Skills

    • At Washington University Nursery School, math skills are honed through activities such as identifying shapes, counting, sorting, and measuring

  • Physical Development: Gross Motor Skills, Fine Motor Skills

    • At Washington University Nursery School, gross motor skills are honed through activities like climbing, biking, and playing with balls and sand, fostering physical development. Simultaneously, fine motor skills are nurtured through writing, drawing, and engaging with manipulative activities like bead stringing and collage creation, promoting dexterity and creativity in the children.

  • Visual Arts

    • Washington University Nursery School places a strong emphasis on fostering children's creativity through visual arts, music, and dramatic play, focusing on the process of exploration rather than the final outcome. The school provides a variety of materials and experiences, such as drawing, painting, printmaking, and 3-dimensional constructions, to enhance children's attention span, fine motor skills, confidence, and independence. Additionally, teachers actively observe, assess, and adapt the curriculum to meet individual student needs, ensuring a comprehensive and tailored learning experience.

  • Extra Help

    • If children require assistance, the curriculum at Washington University Nursery School is flexibly adapted through personalized approaches, including increased teacher-student interaction, expanded opportunities for exploration, simplified or expanded activities, and diversified sensory engagement to cater to various learning styles and address specific needs.
